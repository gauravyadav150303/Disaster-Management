TORNADO

Do's

1. Take shelter immediately in a basement or an interior room without windows.
2. Monitor official weather warnings and alerts.
3. Keep a battery-powered radio or mobile with emergency alerts enabled.
4. Cover your head and neck with your arms or a sturdy object.
5. If you're in a vehicle, drive to the nearest shelter if possible.
6. Lie flat in a low spot (like a ditch) if there's no shelter nearby.
7. Keep your emergency kit ready and accessible.
8. Reinforce windows and doors ahead of tornado season.
9. Help family members, especially elderly or kids, take safe positions.
10. Stay indoors until authorities say it's safe.

Don'ts

1. Don’t stay in mobile homes or temporary shelters during a tornado.
2. Don’t open windows – it won't "equalize pressure" and may cause injury.
3. Don’t try to outrun a tornado in a car unless there's no other choice.
4. Don’t take shelter under overpasses – wind can be stronger there.
5. Don’t ignore tornado sirens or alerts.
6. Don’t use elevators – power may fail.
7. Don’t stand near windows or outside walls.
8. Don’t go outside to film or photograph the tornado.
9. Don’t spread misinformation – follow only trusted sources.
10. Don’t return to damaged structures without inspection.

Essential Kit

1. Flashlight with batteries
2. First-aid kit and basic medicines
3. Drinking water and ready-to-eat food for 3 days
4. Battery-operated radio
5. Power bank and phone charger
6. Emergency whistle
7. Important documents in waterproof pouch
8. Multi-tool and duct tape
9. Gloves and sturdy shoes
10. Blanket or thermal sheets
11. Extra clothes and hygiene items
12. Face masks and hand sanitizer
13. Emergency contact list and evacuation plan

Tornado Resistant Home

1. Build or identify a tornado-safe room or storm shelter.
2. Reinforce garage doors and roof connections.
3. Use impact-resistant windows or install shutters.
4. Anchor mobile homes to the ground securely.
5. Secure outdoor furniture and heavy items during alerts.
6. Store your emergency kit in the safest room.
7. Regularly inspect and maintain your shelter area.
8. Clear debris from around your home to reduce flying hazards.
9. Conduct tornado drills with family.
10. Stay connected to weather alert systems.

Survival Skills

1. Take Shelter Immediately – In a basement or interior room without windows.
2. Get Under a Sturdy Object – Table, workbench, or mattress.
3. Protect Your Head – Use helmets, cushions, or arms.
4. Stay Low and Crouch – Avoid standing near doors or windows.
5. Don’t Stay in Cars or Mobile Homes – They’re unsafe during tornadoes.
6. Use Weather Alerts – Tornado sirens or mobile warnings.
7. Avoid Overpasses or Bridges – Wind speed increases in these areas.
8. After Tornado Passes, Watch for Hazards – Downed wires, gas leaks.
9. Keep Phone Charged – For updates and emergency calls.
10. Stay Put Until Officials Declare It Safe – Tornadoes can come in waves.

Recovery and Resources

1. Prioritize Mental Health – Seek counseling, support groups, or self-care practices.
2. Access Financial Assistance – Government aid, unemployment benefits, and relief funds.
3. Rebuild Social Connections – Reconnect with family, friends, and communities.
4. Get Vaccinated – Protect yourself and others from ongoing or future health risks.
5. Support Local Businesses – Buy locally or donate to help businesses recover.
6. Stay Informed – Keep up with health guidelines and recovery plans from officials.
7. Foster Resilience – Build a routine, stay active, and practice positive thinking.
8. Take Advantage of Education and Training – Upskill to increase job opportunities.
9. Volunteer or Donate – Help those affected by the pandemic with time or resources.
10. Be Patient and Compassionate – Recovery takes time; support each other through the process.
