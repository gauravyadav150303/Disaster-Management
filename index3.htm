CYCLONE

Do's

1. Listen to weather updates and alerts from official sources.
2. Keep your mobile phone charged and emergency contacts saved.
3. Store food and water for at least 3 days.
4. Stay indoors and keep doors and windows securely shut.
5. Turn off electrical mains and gas if instructed.
6. Keep an emergency kit handy with torch, batteries, radio, etc.
7. Move to a safe shelter if your area is declared unsafe.
8. Help neighbors, elderly, and people with disabilities.
9. Tie down or store loose outdoor objects like garbage bins, tools, etc.
10. Stay away from coastal areas, beaches, and rivers.

Don'ts

1. Don’t ignore cyclone warnings — act immediately.
2. Don’t go outside or take shelter under trees during strong winds.
3. Don’t use electrical appliances during a storm.
4. Don’t spread or believe in rumors — follow official channels.
5. Don’t drive or walk through flooded areas.
6. Don’t leave pets behind during evacuation.
7. Don’t light candles or matches if there's a gas leak.
8. Don’t touch fallen power lines or broken poles.
9. Don’t panic — remain calm and help others stay calm.
10. Don’t return home unless advised by authorities.

Essential Kit

1. Bottled water and dry food for 3 days
2. Flashlight with extra batteries
3. Portable radio with batteries
4. First aid kit with essential medicines
5. Important documents in a waterproof folder
6. Power bank and mobile charger
7. Emergency contact list
8. Extra clothes and raincoat
9. Cash in small denominations
10. Hygiene items like sanitizer, soap, tissues
11. Whistle and multi-purpose knife
12. Blanket or thermal sheet
13. Basic tools and rope

Cyclone Resistant Home

1. Reinforce roof structures to resist high winds.
2. Install storm shutters or board up windows.
3. Trim trees and branches near your house.
4. Use cyclone straps or roof ties to secure roofs.
5. Secure outdoor items like tanks, bins, and garden tools.
6. Ensure doors and windows are tightly sealed.
7. Elevate electrical wiring and appliances.
8. Store sandbags to divert water away from home.
9. Repair cracks or leaks in walls and roofs.
10. Identify the strongest room in your house for shelter during cyclones.

Survival Skills

1. Stay Indoors and Away from Windows – Flying debris is dangerous.
2. Keep Your Disaster Kit Handy – Flashlight, food, medicine, etc.
3. Turn Off Gas and Electricity – To prevent leaks and fire hazards.
4. Stay Tuned to Weather Bulletins – Use radio or mobile alerts.
5. Evacuate When Officially Advised – Don’t delay or argue.
6. Store Drinking Water – Water lines may be contaminated.
7. Keep Important Documents Safe – In waterproof covers.
8. Use Heavy Furniture to Block Wind Entry Points – Doors/windows.
9. Mark a Family Meeting Point – In case of separation.
10. Stay Calm and Offer Help – Especially to children and elderly.

Recovery and Resources

1. Check for Structural Safety – Inspect homes for damage, collapse risk, or structural issues.
2. Access Financial Help – Apply for government aid, insurance, or relief funds for rebuilding.
3. Seek Medical Aid – Attend to any physical injuries or seek psychological counseling for trauma.
4. Rebuild Livelihoods – Support local businesses, start rebuilding efforts with government programs.
5. Reconnect with Family – Ensure everyone is accounted for and receive mental health support.
6. Secure Temporary Shelter – Use shelters or government relief programs if home is uninhabitable.
7. Volunteer and Donate – Offer your help in relief operations or donate to organizations in need.
8. Be Aware of Contaminants – Ensure water and food supplies are safe, disinfect affected areas.
9. Keep Communication Open – Stay informed through local media and emergency channels.
10. Support Recovery Initiatives – Participate in rebuilding the community and improving resilience.
