FOREST FIRE

Do's

1. Stay calm and follow official fire evacuation orders.
2. Evacuate immediately if instructed to do so, and move to a safe area.
3. Close all doors and windows to prevent smoke from entering your home.
4. Use a wet cloth to cover your nose and mouth to avoid inhaling smoke.
5. Stay indoors unless evacuation is absolutely necessary.
6. Follow evacuation routes; do not take shortcuts.
7. Keep an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, medications, and a flashlight.
8. Stay updated with fire reports through a battery-powered radio or mobile.
9. Protect pets and livestock; bring them to safety.
10. Alert neighbors and vulnerable individuals to evacuate if needed.

Don'ts

1. Don’t stay in your home if you are instructed to evacuate.
2. Don’t use elevators during evacuation; always use stairs.
3. Don’t drive through areas with heavy smoke or fire.
4. Don’t attempt to fight the fire unless trained and equipped.
5. Don’t block evacuation routes; leave immediately when ordered.
6. Don’t leave pets behind during evacuation.
7. Don’t use open flames or flammable devices during a fire emergency.
8. Don’t drink contaminated water from the fire-affected areas.
9. Don’t ignore evacuation orders; they’re issued for your safety.
10. Don’t attempt to return home until it’s declared safe by authorities.

Essential Kit

1. Bottled drinking water (at least 3 liters per person per day)
2. Non-perishable food (granola bars, canned food)
3. Torch/flashlight with spare batteries
4. First aid kit and essential personal medications
5. Power bank and phone charger
6. Battery-operated radio for emergency updates
7. Wet cloths or masks for smoke protection
8. Extra clothes and sturdy shoes for evacuation
9. Basic hygiene products: sanitizer, tissue, sanitary pads
10. Emergency contact list with local helpline numbers
11. Important documents (stored in waterproof bags)
12. Cash in small denominations
13. Pet carriers and food for pets

Fire-Resistant Home

1. Create a defensible space around your home by clearing dead vegetation and debris.
2. Use fire-resistant roofing materials to protect your home.
3. Install mesh screens on vents to prevent sparks from entering your home.
4. Use fire-resistant building materials for your walls, windows, and doors.
5. Keep trees and shrubs trimmed to reduce fire risk near your property.
6. Store firewood and other flammable materials away from your home.
7. Maintain a firebreak around your property to reduce fire spread.
8. Regularly clean gutters and roofs to remove leaves and debris.
9. Install a sprinkler system to protect your property during a fire.
10. Build a water source, such as a pond or rainwater collection system, for firefighting.

Survival Skills

1. Evacuate immediately if instructed by authorities – Don’t wait until the fire is close.
2. Stay low to the ground to avoid smoke inhalation if trapped.
3. Cover your nose and mouth with a wet cloth to protect from smoke.
4. Stay inside your vehicle if trapped and drive away as soon as it is safe.
5. Avoid using a vehicle if roads are blocked or fire is nearby.
6. Use a damp towel to seal gaps in doors and windows if you cannot evacuate.
7. Stay hydrated, especially if you are in an evacuation shelter.
8. Keep pets in carriers and make sure they are with you during evacuation.
9. Help vulnerable individuals and neighbors evacuate if possible.
10. Stay updated with evacuation routes and fire reports through radio or mobile alerts.

Recovery and Resources

1. Ensure Safety First – Check for hazards like gas leaks, electrical issues, or structural damage.
2. Seek Financial Assistance – Apply for government aid, fire insurance, or disaster relief funds.
3. Rebuild Homes and Property – Work with local authorities for safe rebuilding guidelines.
4. Clean and Sanitize – Properly clean fire-damaged items to prevent hazards like smoke residue.
5. Access Medical Assistance – Seek treatment for burns, injuries, or mental health support.
6. Reconnect with Community – Join local support groups or recovery initiatives.
7. Secure Temporary Housing – If your home is uninhabitable, explore shelters or relocation resources.
8. Donate or Volunteer – Contribute to recovery efforts through donations or volunteer work.
9. Stay Updated – Follow authorities for recovery steps and guidelines in your area.
10. Be Patient – Recovery from forest fires can take time; stay resilient and seek help when needed.
