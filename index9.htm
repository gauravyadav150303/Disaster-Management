THUNDERSTORM

Do's

1. Stay indoors and avoid travel unless absolutely necessary.
2. Unplug electrical appliances to avoid damage from power surges.
3. Stay away from windows and doors.
4. Use a battery-powered radio for weather alerts.
5. If outside, seek shelter in a sturdy building or vehicle.
6. Close all windows and secure loose objects outside.
7. Avoid using water (showers, taps) during lightning activity.
8. Help children and elderly to remain calm and safe.
9. Charge mobile devices before the storm worsens.
10. Follow local advisories and emergency instructions.

Don'ts

1. Don’t stand under trees or open shelters during lightning.
2. Don’t touch metal objects like fences, poles, or wires.
3. Don’t take shelter in open fields or near water bodies.
4. Don’t use landline phones during a thunderstorm.
5. Don’t operate electrical appliances unless necessary.
6. Don’t drive through flooded roads or underpasses.
7. Don’t attempt to take photos of lightning from unsafe areas.
8. Don’t ignore weather warnings or delay taking action.
9. Don’t rely on myths (like rubber tires protecting cars).
10. Don’t spread rumors – share verified updates only.

Essential Kit

1. Torch and spare batteries
2. Battery-powered radio
3. First-aid kit
4. Bottled drinking water and non-perishable food
5. Mobile phone and power bank
6. Important documents in waterproof bag
7. Emergency contacts list
8. Extra clothes and blankets
9. Multipurpose tool and whistle
10. Face masks and sanitizer
11. Emergency cash
12. Medicines for existing health conditions

Thunderstorm Resistant Home

1. Install lightning rods and surge protectors for safety.
2. Trim trees and remove weak branches near your home.
3. Secure or store outdoor furniture and items.
4. Reinforce roofs, windows, and doors.
5. Elevate electrical systems if you're in flood-prone areas.
6. Install water drainage systems to prevent waterlogging.
7. Keep emergency lighting ready and accessible.
8. Have a designated safe room away from windows.
9. Maintain your backup power sources.
10. Train your family in emergency drills.

Survival Skills

1. Stay Calm and Think Clearly – Panic can lead to poor decisions.
2. Identify Safe Zones Indoors – Preferably an interior room without windows.
3. Know How to Shut Off Utilities – Gas, water, and electricity if needed.
4. Practice Basic First Aid – Be able to treat cuts, burns, and shock.
5. Use a Battery-powered Radio – Stay updated on weather alerts and emergency news.
6. Signal for Help – Use a whistle, flashlight, or phone if trapped.
7. Ration Supplies – Conserve food and water if the storm lasts long.
8. Avoid Using Electrical Appliances – Prevent electrocution during lightning.
9. Understand Lightning Safety – Stay low and away from conductors if caught outside.
10. Help Others – Especially children, elderly, or injured during the emergency.

Recovery and Resources

1. Inspect for Damage – Check for downed trees, power lines, or structural damage.
2. Restore Power – Report power outages to local utilities and wait for updates.
3. Seek Medical Assistance – Address injuries or trauma, including emotional recovery.
4. Repair Property – Begin repairs as soon as it is safe to do so, following local regulations.
5. Secure Safe Drinking Water – Make sure your water supply is safe after a storm.
6. Rebuild Community – Volunteer in local clean-up efforts and support affected individuals.
7. Provide Mental Health Support – Seek counseling or help others with stress or post-traumatic stress.
8. Access Financial Aid – Apply for emergency relief funds or insurance claims.
9. Stay Informed – Follow authorities’ guidelines on recovery, including when it's safe to go back outdoors.
10. Prevent Future Damage – Consider stormproofing your home and preparing for future storms.
