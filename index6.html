EARTHQUAKE

Do's

1. During an Earthquake Drop, Cover, and Hold On – Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy table or furniture, and hold on until shaking stops.
2. Stay Indoors if you are already inside. Most injuries occur when people try to move to another location.
3. Protect Your Head and Neck with your arms or something soft like a pillow or book.
4. Stay Away from Windows and Glass – These can shatter and cause injury.
5. Turn Off Gas and Electricity only if you smell gas or see damaged wires.
6. Use Stairs Instead of Elevators – Elevators may become stuck or malfunction.
7. Remain Calm and reassure others around you.
8. If Outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and utility wires.
9. If Driving, stop your vehicle in a clear area away from bridges, overpasses, and power lines. Stay inside until the shaking stops.

Dont's

1. Don’t Run Outside – You are more likely to be injured by falling debris.
2. Don’t Use Elevators – Power may fail, trapping you inside.
3. Don’t Stand Near Windows, Mirrors, or Heavy Objects that could fall.
4. Don’t Light Candles or Matches if you suspect a gas leak.
5. Don’t Panic or Rush – It can cause stampedes or accidents.
6. Don’t Go Near Coastal Areas immediately after the shaking stops—risk of tsunamis.
7. Don’t Use Your Phone Unnecessarily – Keep lines open for emergency use.
8. Don’t Believe or Spread Rumors – Rely on official updates only.

Essential Kit

1. Drinking Water – At least 3 liters per person per day (for at least 3 days)
2. Ready-to-Eat Food – Non-perishable food items like energy bars, dry fruits, canned food
3. Flashlight – Preferably LED with extra batteries
4. Battery-powered or Hand-crank Radio – For emergency news and alerts
5. Power Bank – Fully charged, preferably solar-powered
6. Basic First Aid Kit – Bandages, antiseptic cream, pain relievers, gloves
7. Essential Medicines – For diabetes, blood pressure, asthma, etc.
8. Copies of Important Documents – ID proof, medical records, insurance (in a waterproof bag)
9. Hygiene Supplies – Hand sanitizer, tissues, soap, sanitary pads
10. Multi-purpose Tool – Swiss knife or similar for basic cutting and repairs
11. Emergency Cash – In small denominations and coins
12. Extra Clothes & Blanket – One set of clothes and a thermal or space blanket
13. Sturdy Footwear – Strong shoes or sandals.
14. Waterproof Matches or Lighter – For emergency heating or lighting
15. Small Fire Extinguisher – For basic fire safety

Survival Skills

1. First Aid & Basic Medical Care

  • Ability to treat injuries like cuts, broken bones, and burns.

  • Knowing how to perform CPR and stop bleeding.

  • Using basic supplies from a first aid kit to stabilize victims until help arrives.

    • 2. Situational Awareness & Hazard Recognition


  • Identifying unsafe structures, gas leaks, exposed wires, and unstable debris.

  • Knowing when it’s safe to stay or when to evacuate.

  • Avoiding panic zones like crowded stairwells or blocked exits.

    • 3. Communication & Information Gathering


  • Using mobile phones, radios, or apps to get official updates.

  • Contacting loved ones or emergency responders effectively.

  • Sharing clear and calm information with others in the area.

    • 4. Resourcefulness & Improvisation


  • Finding clean water, food, and shelter if supply lines are disrupted.

  • Using available materials to build temporary shelter or carry injured people.

  • Adapting quickly to new, unfamiliar conditions.

    • 5. Emotional Resilience & Decision-Making Under Pressure


  • Staying calm and focused in a chaotic environment.

  • Making logical, potentially life-saving decisions despite fear or confusion.

  • Providing emotional support to others — especially children or the elderly.

    • Earthquake-Resistant Home:


    • Secure Heavy Furniture and Appliances:
       Fasten large furniture, bookshelves, and appliances (like refrigerators, TVs, and microwaves) to the wall with brackets or straps.

    • Anchor Large Items:
       Anchor heavy items such as televisions, bookcases, and cabinets to prevent them from toppling over during shaking.

    • Install Latches on Cabinets:
       Use safety latches to secure cabinets and drawers, preventing them from flying open during an earthquake and spilling their contents.

    • Retrofitting Your Foundation:
       If you live in a house with a crawl space, consider retrofitting your foundation with steel brackets or additional reinforcement to prevent shifting during an earthquake.

    • Use Flexible Gas Lines:
       Replace rigid gas lines with flexible ones that won’t break during shaking, reducing the risk of fires or gas leaks.

    • Install Shatterproof Window Films:
       Apply shatterproof window films or install tempered glass to reduce the risk of injury from broken glass during the quake.

    • Reinforce Doors and Windows:
       Ensure that doors and windows are properly reinforced and can open easily during an evacuation. Make sure they are securely framed.

    • Strengthen Roof and Wall Connections:
       Reinforce the connections between your home’s roof and walls to prevent the structure from collapsing in a major quake.

    • Secure Water Heaters and Gas Tanks:
       Strap water heaters and other large equipment like gas tanks to walls to prevent them from tipping over during an earthquake.

    • Check the Structural Integrity of the Home:
       Consult with a professional engineer to inspect and upgrade the structural integrity of your home, including the foundation and support beams.

    • Create an Emergency Exit Plan:
       Ensure you have multiple exit routes planned and practice drills with your family. This includes checking that doors and windows open easily and are not blocked.

    Recovery & Resources


    • Know the locations of nearby emergency shelters and medical facilities.

    • Document any structural damage for insurance and repair needs.

    • Prioritize mental and emotional support to help yourself and others recover.

    • Familiarize yourself with government and non-profit recovery programs.

    • Establish channels for ongoing communication with local authorities and community groups.
