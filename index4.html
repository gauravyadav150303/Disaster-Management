FLOOD

Do's

1. Stay calm and monitor weather updates from official sources.
2. Move to higher ground or upper floors if flooding occurs.
3. Turn off electricity and gas connections before evacuating.
4. Keep an emergency kit ready with essentials like food, water, flashlight, and medicines.
5. Drink only clean or boiled water to avoid infections.
6. Help children, elderly, and differently-abled individuals during evacuation.
7. Use waterproof bags to protect important documents.
8. Stay indoors unless absolutely necessary.
9. If you must walk through water, use a stick to check depth and obstacles.
10. Follow evacuation orders and routes issued by authorities.

Don'ts

1. Don’t walk or drive through floodwaters — they may be deeper or faster than they appear.
2. Don’t use electrical appliances if you're wet or in standing water.
3. Don’t consume water or food that has come into contact with floodwater.
4. Don’t spread or believe in rumors — stay informed via official sources.
5. Don’t ignore evacuation instructions from authorities.
6. Don’t light candles or matches if you suspect a gas leak.
7. Don’t leave pets or animals behind during evacuation.
8. Don’t go near rivers, bridges, or large trees during heavy rain.
9. Don’t re-enter your home until it's declared safe by officials.
10. Don’t touch downed power lines — stay far away and report them.

Essential Kit

1. Bottled drinking water (at least 3 liters per person per day)
2. Non-perishable food (dry fruits, biscuits, canned food)
3. Torch/flashlight with spare batteries
4. First aid kit and essential personal medications
5. Power bank and phone charger
6. Battery-operated radio for emergency updates
7. Waterproof pouch with ID proofs and important documents
8. Extra clothes and rainwear
9. Basic hygiene products: sanitizer, tissue, sanitary pads
10. Cash in small denominations
11. Emergency whistle, rope, and multipurpose knife
12. Emergency contact list with local helpline numbers

Flood Resistant Home

1. Elevate electrical systems — including wiring, sockets, switches, and circuit breakers above potential flood levels.
2. Use water-resistant building materials for walls, floors, and furniture.
3. Install sump pumps with battery backup to remove accumulated water from basements.
4. Build barriers (like floodwalls or levees) around your home if it’s in a flood-prone area.
5. Seal basement walls with waterproofing compounds.
6. Improve drainage around your home — keep gutters and storm drains clear.
7. Elevate or relocate appliances such as washing machines and refrigerators.
8. Anchor fuel tanks to prevent them from floating or tipping during floods.
9. Store valuables and documents in waterproof, elevated containers.
10. Landscape with native plants that absorb excess rainwater and reduce runoff.

Survival Skills

1. Move to Higher Ground Immediately – Avoid low-lying areas.
2. Avoid Walking or Driving Through Flood Water – Even shallow water can be dangerous.
3. Disconnect Electrical Appliances – To avoid short circuits or electrocution.
4. Listen to Weather Updates – Use battery-powered radio or phone.
5. Stay Inside Unless Evacuation is Advised – Don’t roam unnecessarily.
6. Use Life Jackets or Floatation Aids – Especially for kids or non-swimmers.
7. Purify Water Before Drinking – Use purifiers or boil water.
8. Avoid Contact with Floodwater – It may be contaminated.
9. Store Essentials Where Water Won’t Reach – Food, medicine, documents.
10. Help Neighbors and the Needy – Elderly or injured may need assistance.

Recovery and Resources

1. Ensure Safety First – Check for hazards like gas leaks, electrical issues, or structural damage.
2. Seek Financial Assistance – Apply for government aid, flood insurance, or disaster relief funds.
3. Rebuild Homes and Property – Work with local authorities for safe rebuilding guidelines.
4. Clean and Sanitize – Properly clean flood-damaged items to prevent mold and bacteria.
5. Access Medical Assistance – Seek treatment for injuries, infections, or mental health support.
6. Reconnect with Community – Join local support groups or recovery initiatives.
7. Secure Temporary Housing – If your home is uninhabitable, explore shelters or relocation resources.
8. Donate or Volunteer – Contribute to recovery efforts through donations or volunteer work.
9. Stay Updated – Follow authorities for recovery steps and guidelines in your area.
10. Be Patient – Recovery from floods can take time; stay resilient and seek help when needed.
