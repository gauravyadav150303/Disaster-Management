LANDSLIDE

Do's

1. Stay alert to weather conditions and warnings from local authorities.
2. Evacuate immediately if landslides are imminent, especially in high-risk areas.
3. Move to higher ground or a stable, safe location.
4. Follow evacuation orders and use designated evacuation routes.
5. Keep away from steep slopes or areas prone to landslides.
6. Stay indoors until authorities confirm it is safe to leave.
7. If trapped, try to signal for help by using loud noises or bright colors.
8. Keep a first aid kit on hand for any injuries.
9. Protect your head and neck in case of falling debris.
10. Stay informed by listening to local radio or mobile weather alerts.

Don'ts

1. Don’t stand near steep slopes, cliffs, or riverbanks during or after heavy rain.
2. Don’t drive in areas prone to landslides or in unstable terrain.
3. Don’t attempt to return to your home or work until declared safe.
4. Don’t try to cross bridges or flood-prone roads if you suspect a landslide.
5. Don’t ignore evacuation orders from authorities.
6. Don’t use electrical appliances if water or debris has entered your home.
7. Don’t disturb or move injured individuals unless necessary for safety.
8. Don’t rely on rumors – always check official sources for information.
9. Don’t approach damaged infrastructure like collapsed bridges or buildings.
10. Don’t drive through areas with debris until authorities clear the route.

Essential Kit

1. Bottled drinking water (at least 3 liters per person per day)
2. Non-perishable food (granola bars, canned food)
3. Torch/flashlight with spare batteries
4. First aid kit and essential personal medications
5. Power bank and phone charger
6. Battery-operated radio for emergency updates
7. Waterproof pouch with ID proofs and important documents
8. Extra clothes and rainwear
9. Basic hygiene products: sanitizer, tissue, sanitary pads
10. Cash in small denominations
11. Emergency whistle, rope, and multipurpose knife
12. Emergency contact list with local helpline numbers

Landslide Resistant Home

1. Build retaining walls around your home in high-risk areas.
2. Use erosion-control techniques to prevent soil movement.
3. Install proper drainage systems to redirect water away from your property.
4. Avoid planting trees or heavy vegetation too close to slopes or cliffs.
5. Avoid building homes in landslide-prone areas, especially near steep slopes.
6. Reinforce the structure of your house with strong materials to withstand pressure.
7. Use geo-textiles or netting on slopes to stabilize loose soil.
8. Keep gutters and drains clear to avoid water accumulation and soil erosion.
9. Regularly inspect the property for signs of soil movement or cracking.
10. Consult with experts to assess the stability of the land before construction.

Survival Skills

1. Move to Higher Ground Immediately – Avoid low-lying areas.
2. Don’t Walk or Drive in Landslide-Prone Areas – These areas are unsafe during heavy rains.
3. Listen to Weather Updates – Use battery-powered radios or phones.
4. Stay Inside Unless Evacuation is Advised – Don’t roam unnecessarily.
5. Protect Your Head and Neck – Shield yourself from falling debris.
6. Help Those in Need – Assist the elderly, children, or anyone who needs help evacuating.
7. Use Emergency Signals – In case you’re trapped, signal for help with a whistle or bright clothing.
8. Purify Water Before Drinking – Use water filters or boil water if necessary.
9. Avoid Areas with Cracked Foundations – These are likely to be unstable.
10. Be Ready to Evacuate Quickly – Keep essentials packed in an emergency kit.

Recovery and Resources

1. Ensure Safety First – Check for hazards like gas leaks, electrical issues, or structural damage.
2. Seek Financial Assistance – Apply for government aid, insurance, or disaster relief funds.
3. Rebuild Homes and Property – Work with local authorities for safe rebuilding guidelines.
4. Clean and Sanitize – Properly clean landslide-damaged items to prevent mold and bacteria.
5. Access Medical Assistance – Seek treatment for injuries, infections, or mental health support.
6. Reconnect with Community – Join local support groups or recovery initiatives.
7. Secure Temporary Housing – If your home is uninhabitable, explore shelters or relocation resources.
8. Donate or Volunteer – Contribute to recovery efforts through donations or volunteer work.
9. Stay Updated – Follow authorities for recovery steps and guidelines in your area.
10. Be Patient – Recovery from landslides can take time; stay resilient and seek help when needed.
